Case Study - I

  • Male, 56 years old
  • Ex-smoker
  • Amyloidosis
  • Hyperlipidemia
  • Previously followed for left leg intermittent claudication, now progressed to CLTI. Urgent arterial duplex US and CTA arranged.

Medical timeline

2015 - Stable angina

  • Imaging revealed multivessel coronary disease (~60% stenosis)
  • Treated medically

2019

  • 2-month history of left foot rest pain and night pain
  • Duplex ultrasound showed:
    • Low left ankle brachial index (ABI)
    • Severe stenosis of left PFA
    • Occlusion of SFA, popliteal, and PT arteries
  • CTA showed tibioperoneal trunk patent with AT artery
  • Patient offered open surgical bypass for CLTI due to extensive multilevel peripheral arterial occlusive disease
  • Underwent semi-urgent revascularization within 2 weeks
    • Post-operative mild wound infection treated with antibiotics
    • Patient was at elevated limb risk due to below knee bypass and CLTI

  • Patient offered open surgical bypass for CLTI due to extensive multilevel peripheral arterial occlusive disease
  • Underwent semi-urgent revascularization within 2 weeks:
